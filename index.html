What is Occupational Therapy ?

Occupational Therapy (OT) is a career based on treating the body and the mind by assisting people to get their lives back on track after an accident or ilness. OTs do this through a variety of treatments, paired with task-orietated rehabilitation. The core skills involve deconstructing activities to understand the root cause of a health problem and after analysis, we provide a personalised crafted solution. In a nutshel; its about finding something that has a meaning for the person, adapt and use it as a rehabiliation tool or goal. We help people navigating from ilness to health and wellbeing.

It is a holistic approach treatment centred on: the Person (wtheir likes, dreams and goals), their Environment (this includes equipment for home and to help access buildings such as rails and ramps), their Health and Wellbeing, Families and Employment/Work

Career Change and a New Experience Ahead

The journey from a suspicious technophobe to learning Python

I'm a person that's always striving and always looking for an opportunity to learn more. For this reason, I've embarked towards this new experience learning at Codenation.

Prior to this I had only experienced technology as a user. When working, I have trialed, tested and used
Eye Gaze and assistive Technology before teaching it to patients.

The experience at Codenation, has been unique and its helpig me to remove some barriers "resistance" to merging technology within rehabilitation. Small aspects such as learning to use Trello, inspired me to develp ways to use it within my own daily routine for produtivity. I am still stubborn, specially as I see them as abstract tools and I prefer more concrete and low techie tools, but now I am starting to consider replacing them.... but I am still testing how effective these tools can be!

This has been an interesting experience in terms of using a wide range of skills including combining research with practical knowledge. From the first day, I have been analysing the materials and learning opportunities including how to adapt to such different skills. I have been reviewing the cognitive demands of learning Phyton as a new acquired skill, the combination of the cognitive demands and the physical effects on sleep patterns and general behaviours. This led me to start searching interesting studies on programmer's disrupted sleep patterns.

Learning Python has been a fascinating journey fullued by curiosity, some coffee and a hate/love relatioship with google search.

I have started to appreciate and value technology more and the impact and possibilities for rehabilitation systems, productivity within healthcare and to improve the patient’s experience.
