Improving productivity with IT  
Unit 1. Outcome 1



1.1 - Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

Modern business's benefit from Information technology (IT) for work tasks as it increases speed, effectiveness and performance of tasks such as scheduling, sharing information, project management, data collection and analysis, storage of information, etc,. These tasks can otherwise be time consuming and use financial resources that can be directed to other areas. IT has also shaped and changed how business operate, and tasks can be done quickly and within a pre-set budget and timeframe. In addition, it is also used for digital marketing, social media and reaching customers easily.

Covid-19 has been a major catalyst for business innovation specially to update to a digital model. The use of IT has been of paramount importance in keeping busines afloat. The office for national Statistics reported the impact of Coronavirus and the drop on productivity levels. Companies "were forced" to innovate, adopt, upgrade and change to incorporate and welcome IT for training/meeting staff working from home and/or different parts of the world, and reaching their customers. Healthcare has also had major shifts and it is now routinely using IT for patient’s online consultations, resources, invoicing and tracking, trace porgress, etc.

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

When completing digital projects such as making a website there are many skills that are required such as time management, problem solving, good planning, organisational skills, professional performance and effective teamwork. Teams should have a good understanding of the expectations for the project, meet work standards, deadlines and understanding of the interdependency of their roles, expectations and the adverse outcomes. There are also issues around individual responsibilities, levels of work productivity and quality (Project Management), (Digital Project Manager).

For example, when building a website some software requirements could be: Pyhton – programming language used to make the website content interactive. CSS – the language that enables to control the style and layout of web pages. HTML – the markup language for creating the website, its used to create, format text, add images, etc.
A project management tool such as Trello can be used as a visual tool for teams to work collaboratively. This allows to define agreed standards including roles, expectations and responsibilities for each team member. Progress and deadlines can also be tracked as well as consistency, productivity and quality. Other important resources necessary are the acquisition of equipment with specifications for the job.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Risks that may impact on digital projects can be due to cybersecurity, computer viruses, file storage and accidental deletion or removal. Other issues can be due to human errors such as lack of definition of the project, or agreed standards with the client, low productivity, inefficient risk management and not meeting agreed deadlines.


1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

After analysing the project and requirements, the technology should match the job and project, if the software matches the job. Other aspects are if the person has experience using the software. This last point includes taking into account if the software requires constant updates for security issues. It is important to choose technology that does not need constant updates as this can impact on the project and the running of the Website.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

Digital projects can be impacted by legal guidelines due to confidentiality and data protection procedures and compliance with the data Protection Act 1998 and the new GDPR and DPA 2018 guidance. The data principles require personal information to be dealt with in adequate, relevant, accurate, up to date, processed fairly, lawfully, secure and not transferred to other countries. The data should be processed in line of individuals and not be kept for longer than necessary.
Organisations should minimise the amount of confidential data collected and should clearly inform of the type of data held, purposes and how it is stored. They must limit access to this data as per informational commissioners’ guidelines (ICO)

Confidential information can be divided in different levels such as contracts, negotiations, business secrets (awaiting for a patent or copyright) and internal sensitive documentation. This also includes copyright material protected by law such as fonts, images, logos, and intellectual property content for websites. Safety and security it’s also a priority as to avoid security breaches. Organisation should self-report to ICO as soon as a breach is identified and if found to be in breach the organisation may be fined.
